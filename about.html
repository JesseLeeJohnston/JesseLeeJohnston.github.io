Growing up in this era really helped prepare me for my career; I remember the screeches of my dial up modem and my sister wanting to use the phone. I remember the sound of “You’ve got mail” and the web based AOL experience. While others were busy interrupting my internet connection I was experimenting with the web; creating websites (with iFrames) and forums about nerdy topics. 

While I don’t have dial up anymore and it’s been a while since I’ve seen an iFrame, I certainly have not lost my passion for the web. I make sure that I’m up on the latest technology by constantly learning. I make sure my clients projects are future proofed to the best of mine and my teams’ abilities. I plan for things that are not only currently happening but things that are going to be happening. I anticipate users’ needs and behaviours. I believe that “cool” uses of new tech things are not always the best solution for the “user”. 

I care about only putting out quality work. Working on the web is not something to hack away at, it's a craft. 

- Jesse
